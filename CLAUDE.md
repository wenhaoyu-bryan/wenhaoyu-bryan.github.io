# CLAUDE.md

## Project Name

**AI PM Personal Brand Website built on Astro Paper**

## Project Goal

Build a clean, credible, fast, SEO-friendly personal brand website based on the Astro Paper theme.

This is **not** an online CV, academic CV, or job-history page.

The website should function as a **high-signal display page** for investors, HR, future collaborators, AI product leaders, founders, and potential clients. The first screen should quickly communicate who Bryan / Wenhao is, why he is interesting, and why someone should continue reading or reach out.

The site should present him as:

> An AI Product Manager who turns ambiguous business problems into AI workflows, ontology-driven systems, prototypes, and growth experiments.

The website should feel more like a **personal product landing page + public thinking hub** than a resume.

---

## Core Principle

Do **not** copy the resume into the website.

The site should not list every school, internship, job, phone number, employment date, or bullet point. It should instead translate selected experience into a clear public narrative:

- What problems he is interested in
- What systems he is learning to build
- What projects he can publicly show
- What methods he is developing
- What kind of people should contact him

The goal is to **catch attention first**, then encourage people to contact him for more details.

---

## Target Audience

Design and write for four audiences:

1. **HR / Hiring Managers**
   - Need to quickly understand his AI PM positioning and evidence of execution.
   - They should see AI product sense, technical collaboration ability, and growth thinking.

2. **Investors / Founders**
   - Need to see curiosity, product taste, market awareness, and ability to turn vague opportunities into prototypes.

3. **Future Collaborators**
   - Need to understand what he is building publicly and where collaboration is possible.

4. **Potential Clients / Side Project Leads**
   - Need to understand SEO/GEO, B2B growth, AI workflow, and website/product experimentation experience.

## What This Website Should NOT Be

Avoid the following:

- Do not make it look like a Chinese recruitment resume.
- Do not expose phone number, exact private contact details, or unnecessary personal information.
- Do not list all internships as chronological job history.
- Do not copy resume bullet points directly.
- Do not show confidential company data, internal screenshots, internal system names, client names, metrics, proprietary business logic, or non-public documents.
- Do not overclaim engineering ability. Position technical ability as **AI PM prototype and technical collaboration capability**, not as a full-time software engineer identity.
- Do not use overly academic CV language.
- Do not make the tone too self-promotional or exaggerated.

## Confidentiality Rules

The owner has private company experience and internal prototypes. These can influence the public narrative but must not be exposed directly.

Allowed:

- Abstracted methodology
- Generalized product frameworks
- Publicly explainable lessons
- Sanitized architecture thinking
- High-level AI PM workflow
- Tool stack reflections
- Public GitHub projects
- Personal experiments
- Side project learnings

Not allowed:

- Internal screenshots
- Internal company data
- Private repo links
- Names of confidential systems unless explicitly public
- Proprietary technical implementation details
- Unapproved metrics
- Customer data
- Exact internal business logic
- Documents copied from company work

Use language like:

> Some work is private due to data and permission constraints. This page abstracts the product methodology rather than exposing internal systems.

## Implementation Rules for Claude Code

1. Inspect the Astro Paper structure before editing.
2. Keep the site buildable after each phase.
3. Prefer small, clear commits or changes.
4. Do not break existing blog functionality unless intentionally refactoring.
5. Preserve Astro Paper's existing SEO and RSS strengths where possible.
6. Add pages gradually.
7. Use Markdown / MDX content where possible.
8. Avoid large dependencies unless necessary.
9. Use simple components for project cards, content cards, and CTA sections.
10. Ensure responsive design.
11. Run format/build checks after meaningful changes.
12. Do not invent unavailable project links. Use placeholders if links are not provided.
13. Do not expose private company information.
14. Do not copy resume text directly.
15. Optimize for a polished V1, not a perfect final site.
